Consumer Protection Act, 2019 empowers consumers to file complaints against unfair trade practices or defective goods/services. 
The Act establishes District, State, and National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions for adjudication. 

A consumer can seek compensation, replacement, or refund for defective products or deficient services. 
The limitation period for filing complaints is generally two years from the date of cause of action. 

Mandatory documents include: purchase invoice, warranty card, and correspondence with the seller. Optional supporting documents may include photographs or videos of the defect.
